Overparameterization:

This problem is taken from [1], example 3.6.

Suppose there is a white noise process:

\begin{equation*} x[t] = w[t] \end{equation*}

Iterating backwards and multiplying both sides by $0.5$ amounts to the same process:

\begin{equation*} 0.5[t - 1] = 0.5w[t - 1] \end{equation*}

Subtracting the two equations obtains:

\begin{align*} x[t] - 0.5[t - 1] &= w[t] - 0.5w[t - 1] \\ x[t] &= 0.5[t - 1] + w[t] - 0.5w[t - 1] \\ \end{align*}

While this looks like an $ARMA(1, 1)$ model, it is still a white noise process. This can easily be proven by substituting $x[t]$ for $w[t]$ or by taking the $z$-transform of both sides:

\begin{align*} x[t] - 0.5[t - 1] &= w[t] - 0.5w[t - 1] \\ \mathcal{Z} \left\{x[t] - 0.5[t - 1] \right\} &= \mathcal{Z} \left\{w[t] - 0.5w[t - 1] \right\} \\ (1 - 0.5z^{-1})X(z) &= (1 - 0.5z^{-1})W(z) \\ X(z) &= \frac{1 - 0.5z^{-1}}{1 - 0.5z^{-1}} W(z) \\ X(z) &= W(z) \\ \mathcal{Z}^{-1} \left\{X(z) \right\} &= \mathcal{Z}^{-1} \left\{W(z) \right\} \\ x[t] &= w[t] \end{align*}

References:

[1] R. H. Shumway and D. S. Stoffer, Time Series Analysis and Its Applications with R Examples, 2nd ed. New York, USA: Springer, 2011.

Proof: Generating an MA(1) Model out of AR

(back to 03. ARMA Models)

(back to index)

We begin with an $MA(1)$ model:

\begin{equation*} x[t] = w[t] + \theta w[t - 1] \end{equation*}

Let's rearrange the equation to solve for $w[t]$:

\begin{align*} x[t] &= w[t] + \theta w[t - 1] \\ w[t] &= -\theta w[t - 1] + x[t] \\ \end{align*}

Iterating backwards once (1st time) gives:

\begin{align*} w[t] &= -\theta w[t - 1] + x[t] \\ &= -\theta (-\theta w[t - 2] + x[t - 1]) + x[t] = \theta^2 x[t - 2] - \theta x[t - 1] + x[t] \\ &= \theta^2 w[t - 2] + \sum_{j = 0}^{1} (-\theta)^j x[t - j] \end{align*}

Iterating backwards again (2nd time) gives:

\begin{align*} w[t] &= \theta^2 x[t - 2] + \sum_{j = 0}^{1} (-\theta)^j x[t - j] \\ &= \theta^2 (-\theta w[t - 3] + x[t - 2]) + \sum_{j = 0}^{1} (-\theta)^j x[t - j] \\ &= -\theta^3 x[t - 3] + \theta^2 x[t - 2] + \sum_{j = 0}^{1} (-\theta)^j x[t - j] \\ &= -\theta^3 w[t - 3] + \sum_{j = 0}^{2} (-\theta)^j x[t - j] \end{align*}

Iterating backwards again (3rd time) gives:

\begin{align*} w[t] &= -\theta^3 w[t - 3] + \sum_{j = 0}^{2} (-\theta)^j x[t - j] \\ &= -\theta^3 (-\theta w[t - 4] + x[t - 3]) + \sum_{j = 0}^{2} (-\theta)^j x[t - j] \\ &= \theta^4 w[t - 4] - \theta^3 x[t - 3] + \sum_{j = 0}^{2} (-\theta)^j x[t - j] \\ &= \theta^4 w[t - 4] + \sum_{j = 0}^{3} (-\theta)^j x[t - j] \end{align*}

Iterating backwards $k$ times gives:

\begin{equation*} w[t] = (-\theta)^{k + 1} w[t - (k + 1)] + \sum_{j = 0}^{k} (-\theta)^j w[t - j] \end{equation*}

If $|\theta| < 1$, then the equation gets reduced to:

\begin{align*} x[t] &= \lim_{k \to \infty} (-\theta)^{k + 1} w[t - (k + 1)] + \sum_{j = 0}^{k} (-\theta)^j x[t - j] \\ &= 0 + \sum_{j = 0}^{k} (-\theta)^j x[t - j] \\ &= \sum_{j = 0}^{k} (-\theta)^j x[t - j] \end{align*}

Thus, an $MA(1)$ model can be written as:

\begin{equation} x[t] = \sum_{j = 0}^{k} (-\theta)^j x[t - j] \end{equation}

Start with the logistic differential equation:

\begin{equation*} \frac{dx}{dt} = x\left(1 - x \right) \end{equation*}

Divide both sides by $x \left(1 - x \right)$ and multiply both sides by $dt$:

\begin{equation*} \frac{dx}{x \left(1 - x \right)} = dt \end{equation*}

We can use partial fractions to split up $\frac{1}{x \left(1 - x \right)}$:

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{x \left(1 - x \right)} = \frac{A}{x} + \frac{B}{1 - x} \end{equation*}

Solving for $A$:

\begin{align*} \frac{1}{x \left(1 - x \right)} x &= \frac{A}{x} x + \frac{B}{1 - x} x \\ \left.\frac{1}{\left(1 - x \right)} \right|_{x = 0} &= A + \left.\frac{B}{1 - x} x \right|_{x = 0}\\ \frac{1}{\left(1 - 0 \right)} &= A + \frac{B}{1 - 0} 0 \\ 1 &= 1 + 0 \\ 1 &= A \\ \end{align*}

Solving for $B$:

\begin{align*} \frac{1}{x \left(1 - x \right)} (1 - x) &= \frac{A}{x} (1 - x) + \frac{B}{1 - x} (1 - x) \\ \left.\frac{1}{x} \right|_{x = 1} &= \left.\frac{A(1 - x)}{x}\right|_{x = 1} + B \\ \frac{1}{1} &= A \frac{A(1 - 1)}{1} + B \\ 1 &= 0 + B \\ 1 &= B \\ \end{align*}

Making the substitution:

\begin{align*} \frac{1}{x \left(1 - x \right)} &= \left. \frac{A}{x} \right|_{A = 1} + \left. \frac{B}{1 - x} \right|_{B = 1} \\ &= \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{1 - x} \\ \end{align*}

The differential equation can now be expressed as:

\begin{align*} \frac{dx}{x \left(1 - x \right)} &= dt \\ \frac{dx}{x} + \frac{dx}{1 - x} &= dt \\ \end{align*}

Integrating:

\begin{equation*} \int \frac{dx}{x} + \int \frac{dx}{1 - x} = \int dt \end{equation*}

$\int \frac{dx}{x} = ln(x)$ and $\int dt = t$, assuming that the constants are equal to $0$. For $\int \frac{dx}{1 - x}$, we make the substitution $u = 1 - x$ and $du = -dx$:

\begin{equation*} \int -\frac{du}{u} = \left. - ln(u) \right|_{u = 1 - x} = - ln(1 - x) \end{equation*}

Now, plugging these solutions all in:

\begin{align*} \int \frac{dx}{x} + \int \frac{dx}{1 - x} &= \int dt \\ ln(x) - ln(1 - x) &= t \\ ln\left(\frac{x}{1 - x} \right) &= t \\ \end{align*}

Taking the powers of both sides:

\begin{equation*} e^{ln\left(\frac{x}{1 - x} \right)} = e^t \end{equation*}

yields:

\begin{equation*} \frac{x}{1 - x} = e^t \end{equation*}

Multiplying both sides by $1 - x$:

\begin{align*} \frac{x}{1 - x} (1 - x) &= e^t (1 - x) \\ x &= (e^t - xe^t) \\ \end{align*}

Collecting like terms:

\begin{align*} x &= (e^t - xe^t) \\ x + xe^t &= e^t \\ (1 + e^t)x &= e^t \\ x &= \frac{e^t}{1 + e^t} \\ x &= \frac{1}{e^{-t} + 1} \frac{e^t}{e^t} \\ x &= \frac{1}{1 + e^{-t}} \\ \end{align*}

Thus, the solution to the differential equation $\frac{dx}{dt} = x(1 - x)$ is:

\begin{equation*} x(t) = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-t}} \end{equation*}

This is known as the logistic function.
